Types of flood warning

We send warnings when flooding is possible, is expected or is happening.

The types of warning are:

Flood alert

  • flooding may be possible
  • usually issued from two days to two hours before flooding might happen
  • this notification is sent to a larger, less specific area
  • ‘be prepared’ if you get this notification

Flood warning

  • there is a risk of flooding to homes, businesses, rail and roads
  • this notification is sent to a smaller, more specific area
  • flood warnings and severe flood warnings are sent to the same area
  • ‘act now’ if you get this notification

Severe flood warning

  • there is a risk of severe flooding and major disruption
  • this notification is sent to a smaller, more specific area
  • flood warnings and severe flood warnings are sent to the same area
  • there is a danger to life if you get this notification

Which areas cannot get flood warnings

Not all locations are in places where flood warnings are issued.

This may be because there are no measurement gauges in that area or because it is an area where not many people live or work.

Your locations may still be at risk even if there are no warnings issued in that area.

If warnings are not issued for your locations you may wish to find other ways to monitor them, such as checking the Met Office forecasts or listening to local radio.
